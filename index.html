You upload a file to Google Drive. Who owns it?

Not you. They do. They can read it, sell it, delete it, or hand it over. The terms of service change overnight. Your vacation photos, your work documents, your backups — all sitting on someone else's computer, protected by someone else's rules.

Blobbed doesn't have servers. We don't store your files. We can't read them. We can't delete them. We can't even see what you uploaded. The encryption happens in your browser. The key stays in your pocket. The data lives on a decentralized network that doesn't know your name.

If we disappear tomorrow, your files stay. If Shelby Protocol shuts down, your files stay. If the government asks — there's nothing to hand over. Zero knowledge. Zero trust. Zero bullshit.

This is what ownership actually means.

01.

The
Stance

Encrypt First

Before your file touches the network, it's locked with AES-256-GCM using a key generated in your browser. The ciphertext travels. The key does not. We cannot decrypt what we cannot see.

"The lock and the key should never meet outside your device."

Own Your Keys

No password resets. No account recovery. No central database of secrets. Your key is embedded in the share link you create. Lose the link, lose the file. That's the trade-off for true control.

"With great privacy comes great responsibility."

Outlive the Platform

The blob registry lives on Aptos blockchain. The actual data lives on Shelby storage nodes across the globe. This website is just a window. Smash the window, the house still stands.

"Build for the network, not the company."

03.

Details

Can you read my files?

No. Encryption happens client-side before upload. We never see the key or the plaintext. Physically impossible.

What if this site goes down?

Your files live on Shelby Protocol nodes and the Aptos blockchain. This website is just an interface. Anyone with the link can still retrieve the file.

Why do I need a wallet to upload?

Registering a blob requires an on-chain transaction on Aptos. This costs a tiny amount of APT. Downloading is completely free and needs no wallet.

Is there a file size limit?

~100MB per file for the MVP. Shelby Protocol supports much larger — limits will increase as the network grows.
